Vision-Centric 3D Perception for Autonomous Driving

I am Yu-Wen Tseng, a Ph.D. student in Computer Science and Information Engineering at National Taiwan University. My research focuses on semantic occupancy prediction, scene completion, and test-time adaptation for driving environments, with the goal of building denser and more reliable 3D machine perception under real-world uncertainty.

Research Overview

Three threads that define my current work

01

Semantic Occupancy Prediction

Studying how vision systems infer dense semantic occupancy from sparse observations in complex driving scenes.

02

3D Scene Completion

Exploring memory, geometry, and contextual reasoning to recover missing structure and improve volumetric scene understanding.

03

Robustness Under Distribution Shift

Investigating test-time adaptation as a practical route toward more reliable perception in unseen and evolving environments.
